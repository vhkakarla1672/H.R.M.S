üìò What Is Medical Coding?

Medical Coding translates clinical diagnoses and procedures into standardized alphanumeric codes (ICD-10-CM, CPT, HCPCS, DRG). These codes are vital for:

  • Insurance claim processing
  • Provider reimbursement
  • Regulatory reporting
  • Data analytics & compliance

  • ‚úÖ Medical Terminology & Anatomy

    Medical terminology refers to the specialized language used by healthcare professionals to describe the human body, its conditions, processes, procedures, and treatments. Anatomy is the study of the structure and parts of the human body.
    üîç Why it's important: Knowing these terms helps coders understand clinical documentation and assign accurate codes.

  • ‚úÖ ICD-10-CM, ICD-10-PCS, CPT, HCPCS Level II Coding Systems
    • ICD-10-CM: Used to code diagnoses and health conditions in all healthcare settings.
    • ICD-10-PCS: Used to code inpatient hospital procedures.
    • CPT: Represents medical, surgical, and diagnostic services primarily in outpatient and physician settings.
    • HCPCS Level II: Codes for non-physician services such as DME, ambulance, prosthetics, and certain medications.

    üîç Why it's important: Mastery of these systems is essential for accurate coding and billing.

  • ‚úÖ Inpatient/Outpatient Billing Guidelines

    Inpatient billing refers to services provided when a patient is formally admitted to a hospital. Outpatient billing covers services where patients receive care without being admitted.
    üîç Why it's important: Different billing rules apply depending on the care setting; coders must follow specific guidelines to ensure accurate claims.

  • ‚úÖ E/M Coding, Modifiers, and Audit Preparation

    E/M Coding involves documenting and coding physician-patient interactions. Modifiers are two-character codes added to CPT/HCPCS codes to provide more detail. Audit Preparation involves reviewing and verifying coding accuracy.
    üîç Why it's important: Mastery in E/M coding and modifiers directly impacts reimbursement and audit readiness.

  • ‚úÖ HIPAA & HITECH Compliance Regulations

    HIPAA: Sets the standard for protecting sensitive patient health information (PHI).

    üîç Why it's important: Understanding HIPAA and HITECH is critical to ensure that health information is coded, stored, transmitted, and handled legally, ethically, and securely.
